# Build-week demonstration

## Two-minute story

The hero user is an owner-operated wholesaler. The demo follows one sale from messy evidence to a validated or repairable e-Invoice lifecycle.

### 0:00–0:15 — Establish the pain

Show an empty review queue. Explain that MyInvois expects structured information, while the owner receives business truth as speech, paper and transfers.

### 0:15–0:35 — Capture

Record a short code-switched voice order: “Ali ambil 20 kotak at RM12, bayar next week.” Upload an order or receipt image and import a supported bank CSV row.

### 0:35–0:55 — Reconcile evidence

Show the transcript and proposed transaction. Open two critical fields to reveal their audio timestamp and document crop. Highlight one deliberate contradiction, such as RM120 in one source and RM102 in another.

### 0:55–1:10 — Clarify

KiraKira asks one focused question. The owner answers, the candidate is regenerated, and deterministic arithmetic passes. This is the primary AI moment.

### 1:10–1:25 — Confirm

The owner reviews and confirms the exact version. Show the audit entry and later bank-payment reconciliation without implying that a model approved either action.

### 1:25–1:45 — Comply

Generate the MyInvois payload, show local validation and submit to the sandbox if available. Poll the asynchronous state. Demonstrate one concise rejection explanation and repaired draft, using a clearly labelled fixture if necessary.

### 1:45–2:00 — Compound the value

Show provisional monthly records and financing evidence completeness, with every figure linked to confirmed evidence and missing coverage called out. End before turning the demo into dashboard tourism.

## What must be live

- Phone-friendly audio, image and CSV capture
- ElevenLabs transcription
- Source-linked candidate transaction
- One ambiguity and clarification loop
- Explicit owner confirmation
- Deterministic local MyInvois validation
- Databricks extraction trace or lineage view

## What may use labelled fixtures

- MyInvois sandbox response when credentials or service availability block access
- Bank data beyond one supported CSV format
- A second month of records used only for the completeness view

## Judge objections

**“Isn't this just receipt OCR?”**  
Receipt OCR handles one artifact. KiraKira reconciles multiple sources, exposes contradictions, asks the owner and preserves field-level provenance.

**“Why not use the free MyInvois portal?”**  
The portal receives structured fields. KiraKira reconstructs trustworthy fields from informal operational evidence before that step.

**“Will AI hallucinate tax data?”**  
Unknown values remain unknown, deterministic rules block invalid drafts, the owner confirms the exact version and submission requires a separate action.

**“Does this qualify businesses for loans?”**  
No. It improves evidence organization and shows completeness; financing providers retain every eligibility and approval decision.

**“Why Databricks?”**  
It provides the governed evidence-to-record pipeline, lineage and evaluation needed to show how an assertion was produced—not merely a dashboard.

## Demo readiness gates

- Day 1: test 20 Malaysian code-switched utterances; if critical values are unreliable, make transcript correction explicit.
- Day 2: prove one known-good sandbox submission or lock the labelled simulator.
- Day 3: every required field in the hero transaction must expose provenance and deterministic arithmetic must pass.
- Day 4: the golden path must complete reliably in under two minutes with vendor timeouts and fixture fallbacks.
- Final day: freeze the scenario and spend remaining time on rehearsal, trust cues and failure recovery—not new features.
