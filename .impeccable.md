## Design Context

### Users

Owner-operated Malaysian businesses in the RM1 million–RM5 million revenue band, often working from a phone while handling sales, receipts, bank transfers and e-Invoice preparation without dedicated finance staff. The interface must make evidence capture, clarification and owner review quick and trustworthy for users with mixed technical confidence.

### Brand Personality

Grounded, reassuring and locally familiar. KiraKira should feel like a careful Malaysian back-office assistant: plain-spoken, competent and warm without becoming cute, chatty or overly automated.

### Aesthetic Direction

A mobile-first, light public-service interface with warm green-tinted neutrals, strong legibility and restrained Malaysian character. Use clear hierarchy, practical density and subtle depth inspired by well-designed government and financial-service products. Avoid generic AI-dashboard aesthetics, neon gradients, glassmorphism, decorative chatbot tropes and layouts made entirely of interchangeable rounded cards.

English is the initial interface language, with i18n-ready layouts that tolerate longer Malay, Mandarin and Tamil translations. Desktop views should use available width deliberately; mobile views should prioritize one-handed capture and review.

### Design Principles

1. Put owner confirmation and evidence provenance ahead of decorative automation.
2. Use plain language and visible system status so uncertain financial data never feels silently accepted.
3. Maintain a clear responsive reading column; no content or action may overflow its container.
4. Use a consistent 4px-based spacing rhythm with tight related groups and generous separation between stages.
5. Keep primary actions obvious while ensuring secondary actions remain accessible and visually quieter.
6. Meet WCAG AA contrast, keyboard access, 44px touch targets and reduced-motion preferences.
