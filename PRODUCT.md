# Niaga Product Context

## Product

Niaga helps owner-operated Malaysian businesses turn everyday evidence—receipt photos, voice notes, spreadsheets, bank statements and order messages—into reviewed transaction records. Those records support clearer bookkeeping, e-Invoice preparation and future financing readiness without asking owners to become accountants.

Compliance is the entry point; trustworthy financial history is the longer-term value. Niaga must never imply that uncertain extracted data, MyInvois readiness or loan eligibility has been accepted by an authority when it has not.

## Primary users

Malaysian micro and small-business owners who:

- manage sales and expenses themselves, often from a phone;
- use receipts, WhatsApp, bank transfers, voice notes or spreadsheets instead of formal bookkeeping;
- have mixed technical and financial confidence;
- need plain English initially, with layouts ready for longer Malay, Mandarin and Tamil translations.

## Core workflow

1. The owner supplies evidence they already have.
2. Niaga prepares a structured draft.
3. Uncertain values are shown beside the fields that need attention.
4. The owner checks, corrects and explicitly approves the record.
5. Approved records contribute to transaction history, document preparation and readiness views.

Provider names and internal processing terminology belong in optional provenance details, not in the primary task flow.

## Product principles

1. Owner confirmation comes before automation claims.
2. Evidence provenance remains available without blocking the editable task.
3. Use familiar owner language: money in, money out, customer, merchant, receipt and bank statement.
4. Never silently accept uncertain financial data.
5. Distinguish clearly between local guidance, saved records and submissions to external authorities.
6. Design mobile-first with WCAG AA contrast, keyboard access, readable metadata and 44px touch targets.

## Interface direction

Niaga should feel grounded, reassuring and locally familiar: a careful Malaysian back-office assistant rather than an AI showcase. Use warm green-tinted neutrals, restrained depth, practical information density and standard product affordances.

Avoid neon gradients, glassmorphism, decorative chatbot language, excessive rounded callouts, tiny uppercase metadata and interchangeable card grids. Use one legible sans-serif family, a fixed rem-based product type scale, a clear responsive reading column and concise progressive disclosure.

## Current scope

- Transaction capture and review from manual entry, receipt, voice, CSV and bank statement sources.
- Owner-reviewed transaction history.
- Invoice and commercial-note creation with readiness guidance.
- Dashboard, reminders, cash flow, inventory, and loan readiness.

MyInvois submission and lender qualification remain future integrations unless a screen explicitly states otherwise.
